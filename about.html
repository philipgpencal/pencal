About Me

I’m a brazilian software engineer who spend the whole day, practically every day, working with .NET technologies, experimenting with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript; dabbling with React and Node.js; and trying to collect potentially usefull information through a few hundred of tech web sites. I build softwares and systems used in diverse companies. I love do it well. I’m curious, and I enjoy work that challenges me to learn something new and stretch in a different direction. I do my best to stay on top of changes in the state of the art so that I can meet challenges with tools well suited to the job at hand. The list of projects I follow on GitHub will give you a good idea of the types of technologies I prefer to be using. I also love to read technicals books, the Robert Cecil Martin books are my favorite ones.

Professional Bio

At the moment, I’m a senior consultant at Altran Technologies in Porto, Portugal. But my career started so far away from here, in the agitated city of Curitiba in Brazil, where I also born and grow up. In September 2010, still in high school, I was looking for an internship and I got the opportunity to work in a tech startup who is responsible for the truck sellers' digital market. I worked there for almost a year where I deeped in the tech world, programming languages, and web concepts. I remember at that time I didn't realize how to dealing with the huge and massive study content to reach my personal goal, become a computer programming.

Since that time, I never stopped to study, five hours week at least. I started to feel that sensation of thirst for knowledge and I realized I needed to worked in a big company to improve my skills. This thirst moved me to one of the biggest HR companies in Brazil, were four good years with many projects, challenges, knowledge acquired. I still remember the happy feeling just after solved a critical bug or completing to building a complex algorithm and see it works and brings benefits to the systems and their users. That's really amazing.

After four long years, I decided to put my career on an international road. I moved to a new company with new challenges, technologies, and the possibility to start to see the English language on the systems. I know it can be little strange, but it made all difference to me. In Brazil, we have not a good basic education on public schools and you must learn English by yourself if you want to reach an acceptable English level. Anyway, after a long year working with complex port systems, I received an invitation to participate in a hiring processing for a developer position in one of the most valuable and rich oil companies in the world.

It's definitely was a game changer in my career, I started to work with teams in different parts of the world, start to understand how big corporations work, the timezones, cultures, rules and process for everything. I really enjoyed to worked there, besides increasing my programming skills, I also improved my English and made good friends. At that time I worked in all types of company, startups, familiar, medium, large and global companies. But I had never worked inside of a foreign team before. So I decided to try, It was one of the most difficult decisions that I ever made, I was quitting a dream job in a big and awarded company with a possibility to make a brilliant career there.

After my decision, I started to work at home for an American digital marketing company. Where I had the biggest technical progression in my career until now, I was deeply involved in new technologies, the blockchain was just a "complex word" when we start to implement a token in a company business model, here I became blockchain expert. I also learned some data science concepts with my high skilled coworkers, It's definitely changed my vision about software architecture. At the beginning of 2019 I decided to reach one of my most ambitious goals, live and work abroad. Everything went well on the interview and here I am on Portugal working for Altran. I usually say god has strange ways to put you in the right path, I think my entire career can be summed up in this sentence.

Personal Bio

I was born in 1991 in Curitiba, Brazil. The capital of the state of Parana, I grew up doing what every kid and teenage does, I played a lot of video games and as a good Brazilian, I also played soccer while studying in a public high school. At the end of high school, I met my true love and with that, I changed my concept of life (Oh yes, so early). I started to create goals lists and planning how I could reach each one of them.

I adopted a dog (one of my passions), got a degree and then a specialization degree in software engineer, I married, started to get interested for finances market as a hobby, data science became fascinating to me. I usually spend some time of my free time studying it.

With my career was progressing we decided to left Curitiba, where It was my home for over 27 years. I'm still fanatic for soccer, Netflix and sometimes I still play video games, some things never change. Now I'm a married man with no children (for now) living in a different country, with a different culture, with 8.508 km between me and my relatives. This can be scared sometimes, but I'm really proud of my trajectory until here. Who knows what the destiny reserve for us, destiny is all.
